Alopecia: Detecting Early for Better Care

Alopecia comes in different forms: Androgenetic, Areata, and Totalis. Recognizing the early signs of each type can help you take action and manage hair loss effectively.

Hair

Androgenetic Alopecia results in gradual thinning along predictable patterns, but targeted treatments like minoxidil or lifestyle adjustments can slow progression and support hair health.

ANDROGENETIC

Alopecia Areata causes round patches of hair loss, often appearing suddenly, but some treatments can help stimulate regrowth and manage the condition effectively.

AREATA

Alopecia Totalis leads to complete hair loss on the scalp, requiring specialized care, but wigs, scalp treatments, and supportive therapies can help individuals adapt with confidence.

What can cause an alopecia?

Genetics & Hormones
Your genes and hormone levels play a major role in hair loss. Androgenetic alopecia is the most common type, gradually thinning hair over time.
Autoimmune Response
When the immune system attacks hair follicles, it disrupts growth. Conditions like alopecia areata cause sudden patchy hair loss.
Nutritional Deficiencies
Lacking key vitamins like iron, biotin, and zinc weakens hair. A balanced diet supports healthy growth and reduces shedding.
Stress - Related Factors
High stress levels can push hair into a shedding phase. Relaxation techniques and self-care help maintain a healthy scalp.
Medical Conditions
Issues like thyroid disorders and scalp infections affect hair health. Treating the root cause can improve growth and reduce loss.
Hairstyling Damage
Tight hairstyles, heat, and harsh products weaken hair strands. Gentle care and protective styling keep hair strong and healthy.
