Exploring Hair Diversity

AREATA

Alopecia areata leads to sudden, round patches of hair loss caused by the immune system mistakenly attacking hair follicles.

AREATA

Care Recommendations:

● Consult a dermatologist to explore treatment options such as corticosteroid injections or topical treatments

● Consider using minoxidil (Rogaine) as recommended by a doctor to promote hair regrowth

● Take Vitamin D, biotin, and zinc supplements to support hair health and immune function

● Consider immunotherapy (diphencyprone) under medical supervision to help stimulate hair regrowth

● Manage stress through techniques like meditation or yoga, as stress can trigger or worsen hair loss

● Protect the scalp from harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or applying sunscreen

● Regular check-ups with a healthcare provider to monitor progress and adjust treatments

ANDRO

Androgenetic alopecia causes gradual hair thinning, typically starting at the temples and crown, due to genetic and hormonal factors.

ANDRO

Care Recommendations:

● Use minoxidil (Rogaine) or finasteride (Propecia) to help slow the progression of hair thinning

● Consider low-level laser therapy (LLLT) to stimulate hair follicles and encourage hair growth

● Incorporate hair-healthy vitamins like biotin, zinc, and Vitamin D into your diet

● Avoid tight hairstyles and minimize the use of heated styling tools to prevent further hair damage

● Explore hair transplant surgery if other treatments are not effective for significant hair loss

● Consider platelet-rich plasma (PRP) therapy, which can help promote hair regrowth

● Regular scalp massages to improve blood circulation and support follicle health

TOTALIS

Alopecia totalis is a severe form of alopecia areata that results in complete hair loss on the scalp.

TOTALIS

Care Recommendations:

● Seek professional advice on advanced treatments such as oral corticosteroids or immunosuppressants

● Explore phototherapy (light therapy) as a treatment option under a dermatologist’s supervision

● Consider scalp micropigmentation to create the appearance of hair follicles if desired

● Focus on scalp health with gentle, non-irritating shampoos and moisturizers

● Look into wigs or prosthetic hairpieces to restore confidence and protection for the scalp

● Support overall wellness with a balanced diet and hair-supporting vitamins like biotin and Vitamin D

● Stay in regular contact with your healthcare provider to adjust treatment plans as needed
